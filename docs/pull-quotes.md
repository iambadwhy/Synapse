# Three pull-quotes

For hero screenshots, launch graphics, Twitter/X crops, and embedded
press. Each one is paired with the exact frame it should overlay.

---

## 1. The capture-to-action promise

> **"Capture should be free. Organization should be automatic. The
> transition from insight to action should be one click — and
> legible."**

**Screenshot:** Map view, mid-motion. Seven clusters in their slow drift,
one cluster mid-hover with its detail card floating. Dark near-black
background, indigo cluster glow, mint accent on the Next Step line.

**Where it runs:** Top of the launch Substack post. Above-the-fold on
the marketing site. First frame of the hero TikTok before the typing
starts.

**Why it works:** it's the thesis, compressed. Three clauses, one
escalation, ends on the ambitious word (*legible*) — the one nobody
else in this category is even attempting.

---

## 2. The trust moment

> **"A black box that decides where your thoughts go is, reasonably, a
> thing you will not use."**

**Screenshot:** Stream view, one capture card hovered, the `Why?`
tooltip expanded showing "People entity (@mayakmakes) + collaboration
intent → People." The rest of the feed is just visible at the edges,
slightly dimmed.

**Where it runs:** Mid-essay, the transparency argument. The "why we
expose the reasoning" section of the design-review deck. Under the
`Why?` tooltip feature callout on the landing page.

**Why it works:** it's the only line that sounds like a user, not a
founder. It names the objection the reader is already forming, out
loud, before they get to form it. That's the sentence that gets
quoted back to me in DMs.

---

## 3. The commitment line

> **"A cluster without a next step is a pile. The Suggested Next Step
> is what separates Synapse from a second-brain."**

**Screenshot:** Action Panel open over a blurred Map view. The panel
shows the Lumen cluster synthesis (typed out, no cursor — the typewriter
just finished) and the Suggested Next Step in its mint card:
*"Draft a 6-week launch-comms timeline today. Put the WCAG 3.0 audit
at week 2."* The Accept button is visibly weighted.

**Where it runs:** The second TikTok (the Next Step feature spot). The
"what makes this different" section of the About page. Any press or
design-community writeup that mentions Notion/Obsidian in the same
breath.

**Why it works:** it redraws the category line. The whole second-brain
space spent a decade optimizing the archive. This sentence says:
archives are piles. We don't need a better pile. We need the pile to
ship one thing.

---

## Usage notes

- Don't over-use all three on the same surface. One per surface, max.
- Attribution in marketing materials is just "Mira / Synapse" — first
  person, no title. The product voice is a person, not a company.
- All three quotes are CSS-typography-friendly — no em-dashes that look
  stupid in narrow columns, no quote marks inside the quote.

# TikTok launch — 15-second spot

**Goal:** drive waitlist signups from the "I have 40 tabs open" profile.
**Format:** single vertical take, screen capture, voice-over, no captions.
**Cadence:** one post per week for four weeks; this is week one.

---

## The spot

**Length:** 15 seconds.
**Frame:** Full-screen Synapse app, Map view, warm desk-lamp ambient audio
bleeding in underneath the VO.
**Mood:** quiet confidence. No hype voice. This is "I figured it out"
energy, not "you won't BELIEVE."

## VO notes

- Spoken flat. No rising inflection on the last line — this is not a pitch,
  it is an observation about your own week.
- Record 3 takes, pick the least enthusiastic one.
- No music. Ambient desk tone only. Silence sells confidence.

## Caption (the single line under the video)

> I didn't file any of this. It just knew.

No hashtags. Hashtags undercut the tone. The algorithm finds this fine.

## Success criteria

- **Watch-through > 75%** — if the middle doesn't hold, the routing
  animation is not carrying its weight. Recut tighter.
- **Saves > likes** — the save-to-like ratio is the signal that the product
  promise landed. A like is "cute." A save is "I need this."
- **Comment pattern to watch for:** "is this real" / "when can I use this"
  — if that's the dominant thread, the product is on-thesis. If it's
  "what's the app" / "what theme is this" we are losing the pitch to the
  visual.

## Four-week arc (for context)

- **Week 1 (this spot):** the reveal. Feature: auto-routing.
- **Week 2:** the Map. Feature: "what does your week look like."
- **Week 3:** the Next Step. Feature: capture → commitment.
- **Week 4:** a single customer cut. One user, one week, real captures,
  real routing. Social proof, minus the "testimonial" staging.
